Strategy Management FAQs

Find answers to common questions about implementing and managing your strategic planning workflow.

How do I set up our Organizational Profile?

The OrganizationalProfile model is your foundation:

  1. Organization Type: Select from Sole Proprietorship, Corporation, Non-Profit, etc.
  2. Sector Classification: Choose from 20+ industry sectors including Education, Healthcare, IT, Manufacturing
  3. Basic Information: Add organization name, address, TIN, and contact personnel
  4. Vision & Mission: Establish your strategic direction statements
  5. Core Values: Define organizational values from our comprehensive values library

Model: OrganizationalProfile, Vision, Mission, Values

How does SWOT Analysis integration work?

SWOT Analysis provides environmental scanning capabilities:

  • Four Categories: Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, Threats
  • Priority Assessment: Very High to Very Low priority levels
  • Impact & Likelihood: Quantitative assessment of each factor
  • Strategic Integration: SWOT factors automatically inform Strategy Hierarchy
  • Periodic Updates: Regular SWOT reviews through SwotReport model

Model: SwotAnalysis, SwotReport

How do I create our Strategy Hierarchy?

Build your strategic framework step by step:

  1. Strategic Perspectives: Define high-level focus areas (Financial, Customer, Internal, Learning)
  2. Focus Areas: Break down perspectives into specific domains
  3. Objectives: Set clear, measurable goals for each focus area
  4. KPIs: Define Key Performance Indicators with measurement formulas
  5. Alignment: Ensure hierarchy aligns with SWOT analysis findings

Model: StrategyHierarchy

How does Stakeholder management work?

Comprehensive stakeholder analysis and engagement:

  • Role Assignment: 30+ predefined roles from Executive to Community
  • Impact & Interest Analysis: Matrix-based stakeholder mapping
  • Engagement Strategies: Multi-select strategies (Inform, Consult, Involve, Collaborate)
  • Risk Assessment: Priority and risk level evaluation
  • Action Plan Assignment: Link stakeholders to Strategic Action Plans

Model: Stakeholder

How do Strategic Cycles work?

Define your planning timeframes and reporting periods:

  • Time Horizons: From Monthly to 10-year planning cycles
  • Cycle Types: Short Term (Monthly-Quarterly), Medium Term (1-3 years), Long Term (5-10 years)
  • Automatic Naming: System generates descriptive cycle names
  • Duration Tracking: Automatic calculation of cycle duration in days
  • Slug Generation: Unique identifiers for URL-friendly references

Model: StrategicCycle

How do Strategic Action Plans connect everything?

Action Plans bridge strategy with execution:

  1. Link to Hierarchy: Connect to specific Strategy Hierarchy KPIs
  2. Cycle Alignment: Assign to appropriate Strategic Cycle
  3. Stakeholder Assignment: Assign responsible bodies from Stakeholder model
  4. Measurement Setup: Define baseline, target, and improvement metrics
  5. Budget & Resources: Allocate financial resources for execution
  6. Automatic Calculations: System calculates improvement needed

Model: StrategicActionPlan

How do InitiativePlannings relate to Action Plans?

InitiativePlannings break down Action Plans into executable projects:

  • Focus Areas: Align with strategic focus areas
  • Dimensions: Categorize by type (Operational, Strategic, Tactical)
  • Risk Assessment: Priority, impact, likelihood, and risk level evaluation
  • Timeline Management: Start/end dates with automatic remaining time calculation
  • Budget Tracking: Multiple budget sources with usage monitoring
  • Resource Management: Track person-hours, equipment, and materials

Models: InitiativePlanning, InitiativeReport, InitiativeResourceItemReport, InitiativeResourceItemPlan

How does performance reporting work?

Comprehensive performance tracking and reporting:

  1. Achievement Tracking: Record actual performance against targets
  2. Automatic Calculations: System computes percent achieved, variance, and weighted scores
  3. Progress Documentation: Capture challenges, successes, and lessons learned
  4. Data Source Tracking: Document data sources and collection methods
  5. Status Monitoring: Track progress from Pending to Completed
  6. SWOT Integration: Link performance to updated SWOT analysis

Models: StrategicReport, SwotReport
